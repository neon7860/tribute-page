⚒ Bobby Moore ⚒


Introduction

Robert Frederick Chelsea Moore (Better known as Bobby Moore) was a professional football player who captained English football club West Ham United for more than a decade. He was also captain of the English team that won the 1966 FIFA World Cup.

He is widely regarded as West Ham United's greatest player as well as cited by Pelé as the greatest defender he had ever played against.


Timeline:

  • 1941 - Born in Barking, Essex.
  • 1956 - Bobby Moore joined West Ham United as a football player.
  • 1962 - Earned a call up to the England squad due to his form and impact on West Ham United.
  • 1963 - Captained his country for the first time. He was the youngest man ever to captain England at the highest level.
  • 1964 - Lifted the FA cup trophy for West Ham United after a 3-2 win against Preston North End.
  • 1965 - Lifted the European Cups winners Cup after beating 1860 Munich 2-0.
  • 1966 - lifted the FIFA World Cup for England
  • 1970 - Was runner up for the European footballer of the year award. Beaten by Gerd Müller of West Germany.
  • 1978 - Retired from playing profesionally.
  • 1993 - Died tragically from Bowel Cancer.
  • 2008 - West Ham United officially retired his number 6 shirt as a mark of respect. 15 years after his death.

    • Of all the defenders I have challenged, Bobby Moore was the fairest, the best and the most honourable.

    -- Pelé
